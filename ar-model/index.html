How to Use

📱 Mobile — Android and iOS

  1. Tap the cube icon at bottom right of model viewer to launch AR
  2. Allow camera, scan a flat surface, tap to place
  3. Drag to rotate, pinch to scale

🖥️ Desktop

  • Drag to rotate, scroll to zoom; auto‑rotates after idle
  • Run on‑device AI to see Top 4 predictions

Context

A fully offline, privacy-preserving AR system that renders 3D models and runs lightweight AI tasks directly on the device, demonstrating data sovereignty and decentralized intelligence.

🔒 Privacy Protection

Camera feed never leaves your device—zero external transmission.

⚡ Edge Computing

3D rendering runs entirely on your device's GPU/CPU.

🧠 On‑Device AI

Quantized MobileNetV2. The viewer can show Top 4 predictions and optional speech output.

Technical Details

On-Device Processing & Standards

  • Rendering: WebGL GPU acceleration for real-time 3D graphics
  • AR Tracking: Local device sensors (camera, IMU) process spatial data
  • Formats: glTF 2.0 (GLB) for web/Android, USDZ for iOS
  • AR APIs: WebXR (Android), AR Quick Look (iOS 12+)
  • Compatibility: Works on modern browsers; AR requires camera + motion sensors
  • AI Prediction: MobileNetV2 (quantized) via onnxruntime‑web.

Connection to Research

  • Data Sovereignty: User data remains on-device
  • Edge Processing: Computation at network edge (your device)
  • Decentralization: Minimal cloud dependency
  • Future Work: Accuracy–efficiency trade-off enhancements & LLM integration for interactive AR
